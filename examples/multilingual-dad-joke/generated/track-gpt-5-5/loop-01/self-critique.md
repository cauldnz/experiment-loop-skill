# Self-Critique: track-gpt-5-5 loop 01

## Candidate inspected
Echo as a bad secret keeper because it repeats everything.

## Provisional generator self-scores
- cross_language_equivalence: 5/5 — The setup, reveal, and mechanism are the same in English, French, Spanish, and Japanese: an echo cannot keep secrets because it repeats them.
- dad_joke_groan: 4/5 — Clean, corny, and affectionate, but it may feel more like a tidy observation than a maximum-groan dad joke.
- brevity: 5/5 — Each version is one compact question plus one compact answer.
- cultural_portability: 5/5 — Echoes and secrets are broadly understandable without locale-specific knowledge.
- translation_naturalness: 4/5 — French and Spanish are idiomatic; Japanese is natural enough, though こだま can sometimes feel more scenic/mountain-like than everyday “echo.”

## Completeness gate
PASS. English, French, Spanish, and Japanese are present. Japanese contains Japanese script. French uses accents and spacing before the question mark. Spanish uses inverted punctuation and accents.

## Main defects
- The mechanism is universal, but “secret keeper” is a social metaphor rather than a visual everyday situation.
- The groan is gentle; it may not produce as strong an eye-roll as a more concrete object behaving badly at a simple task.
- Japanese こだま is correct, but the register may be slightly less everyday than the English/French/Spanish versions.

## Lesson
Prefer a concrete, visually obvious universal property that creates the failure directly, not a property plus an added social role.

## Next hypothesis
Try a shadow joke: a shadow is bad at hiding because it always follows someone. This should preserve cross-language equivalence while improving visual immediacy, natural Japanese, and dad-joke groan.
