## Completeness gate
PASS. English, French, Spanish, and Japanese are present. Japanese contains kanji and kana (`壁`, `隣`, `角`, `で`, `会おう`). French uses required apostrophes/accents and spacing; Spanish uses inverted question/exclamation punctuation and `rincón`.

## Remaining defect
The explicit adjacency repair over-corrects the source: it makes English and French wordier than necessary. The languages also use slightly different tense framing. Natural localization should preserve the mechanism, not force identical syntax.

## Durable lesson
When panel feedback exposes a lexical seam, repair the local term without padding every language with explanatory wording; semantic equivalence matters more than literal structural symmetry.
