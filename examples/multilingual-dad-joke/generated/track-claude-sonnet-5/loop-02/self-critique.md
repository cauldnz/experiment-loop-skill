- **cross_language_equivalence: 5/5.** All four setups now share the exact
  same grammatical shape ("to be" + tired-adjective/participle: I'm tired /
  je suis fatigué / estoy cansado / 疲れた), and all four punchlines use each

- **dad_joke_groan: 5/5.** Same affectionate, corny, eye-roll-worthy beat as

## One lesson
When a comic mechanism depends on a specific grammatical pattern (here,
"to be" + adjective), audit every target language's *idiomatic* phrasing for
that exact concept before finalizing the anchor content-word — near-synonyms
(hungry/tired/cold/sleepy) can differ in which verb they pair with
(avoir/tener vs. être/estar) even within the same language family, and that
grammatical mismatch is often more damaging to cross-language equivalence
than any single vocabulary choice.

## Decision
Keep for synthesis: **yes**. This loop-02 candidate is the recommended final

loop 01, but with an anchor word choice that makes the mechanism land
naturally and equivalently in English, French, Spanish, and Japanese alike.
