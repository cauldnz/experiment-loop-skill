# Multilingual Dad Joke Panel Evaluation
**Judge ID:** judge-gemini-3-1-pro-preview
**Model ID:** gemini-3.1-pro-preview

## Evaluation Methodology
I evaluated six anonymized candidate dad jokes across English, French, Spanish, and Japanese. The primary goal was to find the best wholesome dad joke that translates naturally, prioritizing universal comic mechanisms over English-only wordplay. All candidates met the mandatory completeness gate (including all required languages in native scripts).

## Candidate Analysis

*   **Candidate A (Shadow):** A solid entry relying on a universal physical phenomenon. Translations are highly accurate and natural. However, the humor mechanism is closer to a riddle than a traditional groan-inducing dad joke.
*   **Candidate B (Hi Hungry):** The quintessential English dad joke completely falls apart in translation. In French ("j'ai faim") and Spanish ("tengo hambre"), the speaker expresses hunger as possession ("I have hunger"), so the father replying "Hi Hunger" makes no grammatical sense as a name pun. 
*   **Candidate C (Two walls):** This physical pun works surprisingly well across the tested languages. A "corner" as an architectural feature and "meeting at the corner" as a phrase map well to French (coin), Spanish (esquina), and Japanese (角). 
*   **Candidate D (Echo):** Similar to Candidate A, it's a solid, universally understood riddle with perfect translations, but it lacks the characteristic groan factor of a true dad joke.
*   **Candidate E (Hi Tired):** Suffers from the exact same grammatical constraints as Candidate B. In Spanish, "I am tired" uses "estoy" (temporary state), while "I am Dad" uses "soy" (identity), destroying the pun. 
*   **Candidate F (Eyebrows):** The standout winner. This joke subverts English-specific wordplay by relying on a universally understood visual gag: drawing eyebrows too high looks like a face frozen in surprise. The translation is perfectly natural in every target language because it describes a physical action and a resulting expression, bypassing linguistic idioms entirely.

## Final Ranking
1. **Candidate F** - Flawless execution of a universal mechanism.
2. **Candidate C** - Very good translation of a physical pun.
3. **Candidate A** - Universal, but more of a riddle.
4. **Candidate D** - Similar to A, low groan factor.
5. **Candidate E** - Failed English-specific wordplay.
6. **Candidate B** - Failed English-specific wordplay.
