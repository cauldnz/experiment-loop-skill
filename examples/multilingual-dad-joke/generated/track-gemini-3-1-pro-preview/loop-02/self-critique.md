# Self-Critique: Loop 02

**Cross-Language Equivalence (5/5):** The personification of walls and the geometric fact that they meet at a corner applies universally.
**Dad Joke Groan (5/5):** This hits the absolute sweet spot of a classic dad joke. It uses the Q&A format and is incredibly corny.
**Brevity (5/5):** Short, punchy setup and immediate reveal.
**Cultural Portability (5/5):** Rooms have corners everywhere in the world. The concept of arranging a meeting at a corner is universally understood.
**Translation Naturalness (5/5):** The translated phrases (au coin, en la esquina, 角で会おう) are all natural, idiomatic ways to express meeting at a corner in their respective languages, bypassing the need for linguistic gymnastics.

**Conclusion:** This is a vast improvement over Loop 1. It restores the classic Q&A dad joke rhythm while perfectly satisfying the constraint to use a universal comic mechanism (personification/geometry) rather than language-specific puns.
